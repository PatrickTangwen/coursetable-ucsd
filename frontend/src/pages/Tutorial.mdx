## 1. Start with a Filter or a Search (#filter-search)

The catalog opens empty on purpose — with thousands of courses, it waits for you to narrow things down first. Use the `Search` bar at the top, or any of the filter buttons: `Subject`, `Course Type`, `Units`, `Term`, or `Days`.

<figure className={styles.shot}>
  <img
    src={catalogEmpty}
    alt="Empty catalog page with the Subject filter highlighted"
    loading="lazy"
  />
  <figcaption>
    Nothing shows until you search or filter — start with the Subject button.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Open `Subject`, type a few letters, and check the department you want. Active filters appear as blue chips next to the filter bar; click the `×` on a chip to remove it, or hit `Reset` to clear everything.

<figure className={styles.shot}>
  <img

    loading="lazy"
  />
  <figcaption>

    Fall 2026.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

## 2. Read the Course List (#course-list)

Each row shows the course code, title, instructors, meeting days and times, location, and seat counts. Two kinds of rows exist, and they behave differently:

- **Single-section courses** (e.g. one lecture only) show `Section A` in gray and a blue `+` on the far left. The `+` adds that section to your worksheet in one click, without opening anything.
- **Multi-section courses** show a badge like `9 sections` instead. These expand in place — more on that in step 5.

<figure className={styles.shot}>
  <img
    src={catalogList}
    alt="Catalog list showing single-section rows with plus buttons and multi-section rows with section badges"
    loading="lazy"
  />
  <figcaption>
    Rows with a + are single-section; rows with a “N sections” badge expand.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

> **Tip:** the `Code`, `Title`, and `Meets` column headers are sort toggles — click them to re-order the list.

## 3. Open the Course Modal (#course-modal)

**The whole row is clickable.** Click anywhere except the `+` button to open the modal for that specific section.

<figure className={styles.shot}>
  <img
    src={rowClick}
    alt="A course row with the clickable area outlined, excluding the plus button"
    loading="lazy"
  />
  <figcaption>
    Everything inside the outline opens the section's modal; the + on the left
    adds it to your worksheet instead.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

The modal's `Overview` tab gives you everything at a glance:

- The full catalog description, units, and course level.
- A `Prerequisites` dropdown and a `Catalog ↗` link to the official UCSD General Catalog entry.
- **Schedule Options** — every meeting for the section: lecture, discussion, lab, and the final exam, each with days, times, room, and seats left.

<figure className={styles.shot}>
  <img
    src={modalOverview}
    alt="Course modal Overview tab with description, badges, and schedule options"
    loading="lazy"
  />
  <figcaption>
    CSE-012's Overview: description, units, prerequisites, and the full weekly
    schedule including the final exam date.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

> The modal updates the page URL, so you can copy the link from your browser's address bar and share a course directly. Press `Esc` or click `×` to close it and return to your filtered list — your filters stay put.

## 4. Check Past Grade Distributions (#past-grades)

Switch to the `Past Grades` tab to see how the course has been graded historically — one row per term, going back years. Each row shows the instructor, the average GPA (color-coded), and the percentage of students earning each grade: A, B, C, D, F, plus W (withdraw), P (pass), and NP (no pass).

<figure className={styles.shot}>
  <img
    src={modalGrades}
    alt="Past Grades tab showing a grade distribution table across many terms"
    loading="lazy"
  />
  <figcaption>
    40 terms of history for CSE-012 — compare instructors before you pick a
    section.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

> **Tip:** the same course can grade very differently under different instructors. Scan the GPA column next to the instructor names before deciding which section to take.

## 6. Match Sections to TSS (#section-mapping)

The third tab, `Section Mapping`, translates SunGrid's section names into the exact TSS section numbers UCSD uses for enrollment (e.g. `001-000-PR + 001-001-OT`). When registration opens, this is the tab that tells you precisely what to enroll in on TSS.

From the modal, hit the big blue `+ Add Section … to Worksheet` button at the bottom of any offering group. (For single-section courses, the `+` on the catalog row does the same thing in one click.)

## 8. See It on the Worksheet Page (#worksheet-page)

Click `Worksheet` in the top-right navigation. Every course you added lands here, laid out as a weekly calendar:

- **Calendar / List** toggle — see your week visually or as a compact list.
- **Regular / Finals** toggle — switch between the weekly schedule and finals week.
- **Stats panel** — planned courses, total credits with a workload label, and a conflict checker that flags overlapping times.
- **Extras** — days until your first exam, your busiest day, and the full course list with per-course details.

In Calendar view, the course cards on the right are collapsible. Click the `⌄` chevron on a card (or `Expand all` above the list) to see the full schedule for that course — lecture, discussion, and the final exam with its countdown — plus a `Remove` button.

### Spot schedule conflicts

SunGrid checks your picks for overlaps the moment you add them. It starts in the catalog: once a course is on your worksheet, any other course that overlaps it gets a red `!` next to its `+` — hover it to see exactly which course it collides with.

<figure className={styles.shot}>

    loading="lazy"

    CSE-020 overlaps CSE-100, so its add button carries a warning — hover to see
    the details.

</figure>

You can still add the course (conflicts never block you). On the Worksheet, the `Conflicts` card lights up with the issue count, the affected cards get a `⚠` badge, and the overlapping blocks sit side by side on the calendar.

### Check your finals

The `Exam` card counts down to your first final. Click it to open the `Exams` overlay — every final on your worksheet with its date, time, and room, sortable by date or by course. (The `Regular / Finals` toggle at the top shows the same thing on the calendar.)

### List view and weekly load

Switch to `List` in the top navigation for a compact list of your courses — and a **Weekly load** chart that stacks class hours per day, color-coded by course. It makes lopsided schedules obvious at a glance, so you can balance heavy and light days before enrollment.
